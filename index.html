The Education Crisis


"The real safeguard of democracy, therefore, is education." - Franklin D. Roosevelt

Teacher Shortage

  • 86% of public schools are struggling to find people to educate their students.
  • Very few teaches are satisfied with their jobs
  • More than a third of teachers are planning to quit
  • Most teachers don't want their children to become teachers
  • Teachers' average weekly salaries have only increased by 29$ in comparison to other college graduates increase by $445 in response to inflation

Declining Student Performance

  • Standardized Testing generates enormous stress among students and impedes actual learning experiences
  • Less than 10% of districts meet the National Association of School Psychologists' recommended number of one school psychologist per 500 students
  • COVID Pandemic had a huge negative impact on student learning
  • 25% of 4th Graders below basic math level (2022)
  • 38% of Eigth graders below reading level (2022)

Inequality in Funding

  • The funding a school receives is based on the property tax value of a district, meaning more affluent areas get better funding
  • The poorest 25% of school districts received 15.6% less funding per student from state and local governments compared to the richest 25% of school districts in 2011-2012.
  • According to the most updated data, federal sources provide only 8% of the funding for public schools, while 47% comes from the state and 45% comes from local sources

What can we do?

  • As a parent/friend: Offer advice, and listen to the students to understand their struggle
  • Schools should offer more accessible counseling services
  • The learning system must move away from standardized because it is innacurate
  • Government programs to provide more funding for schools in poorer district
